# Triangulation realization to polygon quotient homeomorphism

For an incidence-certified finite geometric triangulation, map each point of an abstract marked
triangle to the corresponding point of its embedded geometric triangle. Prove that the map is
constant exactly along the side pairings, descend it to the polygonal quotient, and prove the
descended map is a homeomorphism onto the surface.

Compact-to-Hausdorff arguments should discharge the final homeomorphism once continuity,
surjectivity from triangle coverage, and injectivity from the geometric common-face intersection
property are established. This theorem replaces the current reflexive legacy bridge after the
realization cutover.
